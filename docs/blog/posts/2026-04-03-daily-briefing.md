## 4. Science

### Artemis II: First Crewed Lunar Mission Since Apollo 17

NASA's Artemis II mission launched from Kennedy Space Center at 6:35 p.m. EDT on 1 April 2026, sending four astronauts — Commander Reid Wiseman, Pilot Victor Glover, Mission Specialist Christina Koch, and Canadian Space Agency astronaut Jeremy Hansen — on a ten-day free-return trajectory around the Moon. It is the first crewed mission beyond low Earth orbit since Apollo 17 in December 1972 and marks multiple firsts: Glover is the first person of colour, Koch the first woman, and Hansen the first non-US citizen to travel beyond low Earth orbit. The mission will set records for both distance from Earth and atmospheric reentry speed (~25,000 mph). Artemis II is intended to pave the way for a crewed lunar landing near the south pole.

## 11. Today I Learned

### Snow Flies Can Both Produce Antifreeze *and* Generate Their Own Body Heat

Snow flies — wingless crane flies found in alpine and subarctic environments — survive freezing temperatures through two remarkable mechanisms simultaneously: they produce antifreeze proteins that physically block ice crystal formation inside their cells, *and* they are capable of generating their own internal body heat through metabolic processes. Most insects are considered ectothermic (cold-blooded) and cannot produce meaningful internal heat, making snow flies a rare and striking exception. Scientists are still working to fully characterise the biochemical pathways that allow them to do both at once, and the findings may have implications for cryopreservation research.
