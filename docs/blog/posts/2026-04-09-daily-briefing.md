## 4. Science

**Cells Have Internal "Trade Winds" — And It Could Explain Cancer Spread**

Scientists at Oregon Health & Science University have discovered that cells actively create directional internal fluid currents — described as "trade winds" — to rapidly push actin and other proteins toward the leading edge where movement and repair occur. The finding, published in *Nature Communications*, overturns the assumption that protein distribution inside cells happens mainly through passive diffusion. Using an advanced imaging tool called iPALM, the team showed these flows are far faster than diffusion alone could achieve. Researchers believe the discovery opens new directions for understanding how aggressive cancers metastasise, and may eventually inform drug delivery and tissue repair strategies.

## 5. Technology

**Artemis II Crew Nears Lunar Flyby — First Crewed Moon Pass Since 1972**

NASA's Artemis II mission, launched 1 April aboard the Space Launch System, is approaching its closest lunar pass this week with four astronauts aboard the Orion spacecraft. The 10-day mission does not include a landing, but the crew has already photographed lunar terrain no human eye has seen directly — imagery described by NASA as providing a scientific baseline for future surface missions. It is the first crewed mission beyond low Earth orbit since Apollo 17 in December 1972. ## 11. Today I Learned

**The "Pirate Accent" Was Invented by a Film Actor in 1950**

The exaggerated "Arrr, matey" accent most people associate with pirates has no basis in historical record — it was invented by British actor Robert Newton for the 1950 Disney film *Treasure Island*, and became so culturally embedded through subsequent films and TV that it is now treated as authentic. Actual pirates of the 17th and 18th centuries came from dozens of nations and spoke in accents reflecting their origins, from the Caribbean to West Africa to the British Isles. Newton's particular West Country (Bristol) lilt became the template, arguably making one regional English accent the unofficial voice of global piracy mythology.
