## 11. Today I Learned — Annapolis Residents Burn Their Socks Every Year to Welcome Sailing Season

Every spring, residents of Annapolis, Maryland gather for an unofficial community ritual: burning their socks. The tradition marks the unofficial start of the Chesapeake Bay sailing season — the idea being that once it's warm enough to sail, you no longer need socks, and you burn them ceremonially to commit to summer. It's been going for years and draws an enthusiastic crowd of locals and sailors. It's a good reminder that some of the most enduring community rituals are entirely self-invented and completely absurd by design.

**Why it's interesting:** In an era when "tradition" is often invoked for serious political or religious purposes, the sock-burning ceremony is a counterexample — a tradition created in living memory, for no reason other than collective joy, that has nonetheless taken genuine hold in a community.
