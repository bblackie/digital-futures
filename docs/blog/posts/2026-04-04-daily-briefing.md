Scientists working in a South American rainforest canopy have identified a new termite species whose body shape — including a rounded, bulbous head and elongated torso — bears a striking resemblance to the sperm whale (*Physeter macrocephalus*). The resemblance is coincidental from an evolutionary standpoint, as the termite's morphology is linked to its defensive and foraging adaptations, not convergent mimicry of marine mammals. The discovery illustrates how much undescribed biodiversity remains in forest canopy ecosystems, which are among the least surveyed habitats on Earth.
