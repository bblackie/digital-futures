### Mars Held an Intense Sandstorm 3 Billion Years Ago — New Evidence

Research published in the journal *Geology* presents evidence of a massive ancient sandstorm that swept through Mars' Gale Crater approximately three billion years ago, preserved as distinctive ripple marks in sedimentary deposits. The finding indicates Mars' atmosphere was significantly denser at that time than the thin, near-vacuum that exists today, offering new clues about the planet's atmospheric evolution and past habitability potential. The discovery was described as serendipitous, spotted in imagery from the Curiosity rover. Separately, astronomers have identified what may be the most metal-poor star yet known — SDSS J0715−7334 — which is more chemically pristine than even the earliest galaxies detected by the Webb telescope, potentially offering a window into the universe's first stellar generation.

## 11. Today I Learned

**Clownfish shrink during heatwaves to survive.**

Scientists have discovered that clownfish — yes, Nemo — can temporarily reduce their body size by 1–2% during marine heatwaves. The mechanism is an adaptive response to heat stress: a smaller body requires less oxygen and energy, increasing the fish's survival rate by nearly 78% compared to those that do not shrink. The finding, reported in early 2026, upends assumptions about fish responses to warming oceans and suggests some species may have more physiological flexibility than previously understood.
