### The Kākāpō — the World's Heaviest Parrot — May Have Had Flying Ancestors

Fossils uncovered in a New Zealand cave have yielded 16 species, including the remains of a previously unknown ancestor of the kākāpō — the critically endangered, flightless, nocturnal parrot native to New Zealand — that scientists believe may have been capable of flight. The modern kākāpō, which can weigh up to 4 kg and is the heaviest parrot on Earth, is entirely flightless, using its vestigial wings mainly for balance. The finding suggests the loss of flight happened more recently in the kākāpō lineage than previously assumed, offering a new window into how island isolation drives the evolution — and loss — of flight in birds.
