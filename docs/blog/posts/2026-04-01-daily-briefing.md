## 11. Today I Learned

### The Pirate "Arrr" Accent Was Invented by a 1950s Disney Film Actor

The exaggerated pirate accent instantly recognised worldwide — the gravelly voice, rolled R's, and "Arrr matey" — has no historical basis in actual piracy. It was essentially invented by British actor Robert Newton for the 1950 Disney film *Treasure Island*, in which he played Long John Silver using a heavily stylised West Country (Bristol-area) English dialect. Because the film was widely watched and aimed at children, that voice became the default sound of pirates in popular culture and has persisted for over 75 years. Real 17th and 18th century pirates spoke in whatever accent they grew up with — working-class English, Welsh, Caribbean, West African, or otherwise — with no shared theatrical register whatsoever.
