## 4. Science

### Artemis II Crew Halfway to Moon in Historic Crewed Lunar Flyby

NASA's Artemis II mission — launched 1 April 2026 from Kennedy Space Center — has now left Earth orbit and is en route to the Moon, marking the first crewed lunar voyage since Apollo 17 in December 1972. The four-person crew includes NASA astronauts Reid Wiseman, Victor Glover, and Christina Koch, alongside Canadian Space Agency astronaut Jeremy Hansen. The mission sets multiple firsts: Glover is the first person of colour, Koch the first woman, and Hansen the first non-US citizen to travel beyond low Earth orbit. The 10-day mission will fly around the Moon and return to Earth without landing, serving as a critical human test of the Orion spacecraft ahead of eventual lunar surface missions.
