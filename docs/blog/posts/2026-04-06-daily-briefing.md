### Artemis II Astronauts Conduct First Crewed Lunar Flyby in Over 50 Years

NASA's Artemis II crew — Commander Reid Wiseman, Pilot Victor Glover, Mission Specialist Christina Koch, and Canadian astronaut Jeremy Hansen — launched aboard the Orion spacecraft on 1 April and completed the translunar injection burn on 2 April. On Monday 6 April, the four astronauts are conducting a planned lunar flyby, becoming the first people to see parts of the Moon's far side with their own eyes and photographing surface areas as Orion loops around before heading back toward Earth, with splashdown expected off San Diego within days. It is the first crewed mission to fly to the Moon since Apollo 17 in 1972. The mission is focused on testing the Orion spacecraft under operational conditions rather than landing. Separately, a new study published in the journal *Geology* presents evidence of an ancient and intense sandstorm that swept through Mars' Gale crater more than three billion years ago, with serendipitously discovered ripple patterns offering new clues about the red planet's prehistoric atmosphere.

## 11. Today I Learned

**Your microplastics data might be contaminated by your gloves.**

A study from the University of Michigan found that nitrile and latex laboratory gloves — the standard protective equipment worn during microplastics research — release tiny particles called stearates that closely resemble microplastic particles. These particles contaminate samples during testing, potentially inflating pollution estimates in published research. It raises questions about the reproducibility of a substantial body of microplastics science and underscores a broader challenge in environmental research: the tools of measurement can become sources of the very contamination being measured.
